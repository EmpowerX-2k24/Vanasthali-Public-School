Pragya Prasun
Acid Attack Survivor
Incident Details

Pragya Prasun from Varanasi was the victim of an acid attack in 2006 by a man who had been stalking her. After surviving the attack, she became a strong advocate for acid attack survivors, working with NGOs and raising awareness. Her efforts helped bring about stricter laws regulating acid sales and increased compensation for victims.

Durga Shakti Nagpal
Harassmant at Work Site
Incident Details

Durga Shakti Nagpal, an IAS officer, took strong action against illegal sand mining in Uttar Pradesh, which led to her suspension by the state government. This was seen as retaliation, as her crackdown disrupted a profitable illegal business. Following widespread public outrage and media attention, the government revoked her suspension within two months, highlighting the pressures bureaucrats face when combating corruption.

Rupan Deol Bajaj
Improper Behaviour and Molestation
Incident Details

In 1988, Rupan Deol Bajaj, a senior IAS officer, accused Punjab Police Chief KPS Gill of inappropriate behavior and molestation during an official party. Bajaj filed a criminal case under Sections 354 and 509 of the Indian Penal Code, dealing with assault on a woman and outraging her modesty. After a long legal battle, the Supreme Court in 1996 convicted Gill, fining him and sentencing him to three months in prison, which was later suspended. This case set a strong precedent for women's rights in the workplace, especially regarding harassment by senior officials.
